Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2025.04.18

At Smashchats, your privacy is not just a feature — it's a foundational principle. Our app and protocols are built from the ground up to ensure that you own your data, not us. This privacy policy explains what limited data we collect (if any), how we handle it, and how our decentralized, peer-to-peer architecture protects your personal information.

🛡️ Core Privacy Principles

  • We can’t read your messages: All communication is end-to-end encrypted. Only you and your intended recipients can decrypt your messages. We never have access.
  • We don’t run your servers: Smashchats is a decentralized client. Like an email client, you connect to servers you control or trust. You bring your own credentials and infrastructure.
  • We don’t store user data: We don’t manage your identity, messages, or content. Your data lives on your device and/or the servers you choose. We can’t access or track it.
  • Analytics are strictly opt-in and anonymized: If you choose to help us improve the app, we may collect anonymous usage data like app crashes, load times, or feature usage. No identifiable information is collected or stored.

📊 Optional Analytics

If you opt in, we may collect:

  • Device type and OS (for UI compatibility)
  • Anonymous usage patterns (e.g., feature usage frequency)
  • Crash logs (to fix bugs faster)

We do not collect:

  • IP addresses
  • Message content
  • Usernames or contact lists
  • Location data

Analytics are handled using privacy-focused tools and stored without any personal identifiers. You can opt out at any time in the app settings.

🔐 Smash Identity & Neighborhoods

Your Smash identity is created and stored locally on your device. It is never sent to us.

When you join a neighborhood, you’re interacting with that neighborhood’s infrastructure, not ours. Each neighborhood may have its own privacy practices — please review those independently.

👥 Third-Party Servers

Because Smashchats is a client, your data may interact with servers or infrastructure you or your community choose. These third parties are not under our control, and you are responsible for selecting trusted providers.

We recommend using self-hosted or vetted community servers aligned with your privacy needs.

🔧 Developer Contributions

If you contribute to Smashchats or related protocols:

  • We may retain your GitHub username or email for collaboration and credit purposes
  • Contributions to public repositories are, by nature, public
  • Internal discussions in the Smash Developers neighborhood are visible to members

All contributor data is used solely for coordination, versioning, and access control within development processes.

📫 Contact

If you have questions or privacy concerns, reach out to us:

Email: privacy@smashchats.com

We’re committed to transparency, openness, and respect for your digital autonomy.

🔄 Changes to this Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. When we do, we’ll post the updated version here and update the “Last updated” date at the top of the page.

For major changes, we will announce them on our website or in the app (if opted in).

Thank you for trusting Smashchats — your data, your rules.
